Sockeye Behavior Reference
SEASONAL TIMING — Bristol Bay Sockeye
Early run (Kvichak, Naknek): June 10–25
Main run (Nushagak, Egegik): June 25 – July 10
Late run (Ugashik, Wood R., Togiak): July 5–20
WEATHER & BEHAVIOR
Falling baro (>1 hPa/hr): fish active, ride shallower — prime condition
Rising baro after storm: fish suppress 12-24hr — reduced activity
SW wind 15-25kt: fish pushed into NE bar faces — classic Bristol Bay condition
NE wind (onshore): fish pushed offshore, work deeper outer edges
Fog/overcast: fish tend shallower, 2-5ft — top of net is key
TIDE & CANALIZATION
Peak flood (2-3 hr in): fish stack on upstream bar faces — highest density
Mid-ebb: fish release from bars into channel edges — wider spread
Slack (±45min): fish suspend, redistribute — lower catch rate
Spring tide (>24ft): bars buried, fish open water channels
Neap tide (<16ft): bars exposed longer — extended fishing window on edges
DEPTH & WATER COLUMN
SST 46-52°F: fish 4-10ft — optimal thermal layer for Bristol Bay
SST <44°F: fish hold 6-12ft, tight to bottom features
SST >56°F: fish ride 2-6ft, near surface — watch cork line
Solunar MAJOR period: peak feeding activity, fish more aggressive — up to 30% increase
TURBIDITY & CONDITIONS
Rain event (last 24hr): fish concentrate near river mouths in freshwater plume
High turbidity: fish school tighter, hold shallower, less gear-shy — better net fishing
Clear water (dry spell): fish scatter, go deeper, more likely to see net — spread effort
Jellyfish: None/Light = fish full water column · Moderate = avoid <8ft · Heavy = avoid <12ft
After heavy rain: plume boundary at 1-fathom edge is prime staging area
MIGRATION CORRIDORS
Nushagak: Wood R. + Igushik → west bay | Nushagak R. → east bay
Naknek: Kvichak + Alagnak → east (Kvichak Bay) | Naknek → central/west
Egegik: N. Peninsula stocks → outer south | Egegik → central
